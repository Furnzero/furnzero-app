Our Story
In a world where the furniture industry is essential for creating comfort in our spaces, the industry's impact on the environment is often overlooked. In the Netherlands alone, millions of kilograms of furniture are discarded annually, with only 10% recycled. The rest ends up in landfills, contributing to a growing environmental burden.
FurnZero was founded as a response to this crisis. Founded by Prize Dennise, an interior and furniture designer dedicated to environmental stewardship together with Luke Ndatigh, a software engineer committed to innovation. Our journey began as we witnessed a large amount of discarded furniture in the streets of Amsterdam and identified the severe environmental toll this furniture waste takes on landfills in the Netherlands. With the new EU laws (EPR, Polluter-pays), EU countries are required to have an effective quality control and traceability system for municipal waste landfills. The responsibility now lies heavily on manufacturers and designers, who must rethink product lifecycles, material choices and end-of-life disposal.
FurnZero was created to ensure no more furniture goes to waste to reduce the amount of furniture waste ending up in landfills. By optimising AI and blockchain, we provide digital solutions that ensure transparency in sustainability efforts, end-to-end waste solutions and responsible supply chains. Our goal is to help the Dutch furniture industry meet EU regulations and embrace a more responsible approach to waste disposal.

Competitive advantage
  • A closed-loop circular system covering the sourcing, disposal and repurposing process
  • Integrated circular material alternatives for furniture production
  • High switching costs from large sustainability data collection saving business 70% of value time.
  • Furniture waste is 95% cheaper than sourced raw materials
Our journey is two-fold
The first phase will focus on the Data portal: Engaging supply chain stakeholders in the furniture industry to analyse carbon footprint, life cycle assessment, material composition, waste generation, product lifespan and environmental certifications. This would in turn set the stage for broader sustainability efforts across the Dutch furniture industry.
In the second phase we will focus on the End-to-end furniture repurposing platform: By leveraging the foundation laid by the open data portal we will then develop this platform mainly for furniture waste collection, repurposing and resale.
